# JEE Rank Accelerator Blueprint

## Product Promise

This is not a notes pack.

This is a 90-day operating system for a student who wants a higher JEE score without wasting time on random effort.

The goal is simple:

- Know what to study
- Know what to ignore
- Know how to recover from backlog
- Know how to analyze mistakes
- Know how to turn effort into marks

## Who This Is For

- Students with 3 to 6 months left before JEE
- Students stuck in the 90 to 150 score range
- Students who study hard but do not see score movement
- Students drowning in backlog, revision gaps, and poor test analysis

## Outcome

By the end of this system, the student should have:

- A chapter priority order
- A weekly study routine
- A PYQ workflow
- A test analysis loop
- A backlog elimination plan
- A realistic score-jump roadmap

## Module 1: 90-Day Rescue Framework

### 1.1 The Rescue Rule

If only 90 days remain, the student cannot study everything equally.

The job is to:

1. Protect high-yield chapters
2. Stop perfectionism
3. Build a revision-heavy routine
4. Shift from passive studying to question-led learning

### 1.2 Backlog Recovery Method

Use this four-bucket method:

- `Bucket A: Strong + Scoring`
  Chapters already familiar and highly tested. Revise and convert into marks fast.
- `Bucket B: Weak + High ROI`
  Chapters that repeatedly appear in PYQs and tests. These get aggressive focus.
- `Bucket C: Manageable + Medium ROI`
  Chapters that can be brought to decent level with limited time.
- `Bucket D: Low ROI or Time Sink`
  Chapters that consume huge effort for low return. Keep these last.

### 1.3 Chapter Priority Matrix

#### Physics

- High ROI: Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Modern Physics, Ray Optics, Units and Dimensions, Semiconductors
- Medium ROI: Magnetism, EMI, Waves, Thermodynamics, SHM
- Time sink if weak: Full rotational depth, advanced fluid mechanics, very niche mixed mechanics

#### Chemistry

- High ROI: Chemical Bonding, Coordination Compounds, Electrochemistry, Thermodynamics, Mole Concept, GOC, Biomolecules, Polymers
- Medium ROI: Equilibrium, Kinetics, d-and-f block, Aldehydes Ketones Carboxylic Acids
- Time sink if weak: Deep salt analysis memorization without system, low-frequency exception-heavy pockets

#### Maths

- High ROI: Functions, Matrices, Determinants, Probability, Vectors, 3D Geometry, Straight Lines, Circles, Quadratic Equations
- Medium ROI: Sequence and Series, Binomial, Limits, Continuity, Definite Integration basics
- Time sink if weak: Very advanced conics depth, highly tricky inequalities, obscure algebraic manipulations

### 1.4 What To Study If Only 3 Months Remain

#### Month 1

- Fix basics of top ROI chapters
- Start daily PYQ solving
- Create mistake log from day one
- Give 2 tests per week

#### Month 2

- Increase mixed practice
- Add timed section tests
- Revise formulas and reactions daily
- Convert weak areas into serviceable areas

#### Month 3

- Full-length mocks
- Heavy revision
- Error reduction
- Speed and question selection training

### 1.5 Score Jump Roadmap

#### Example: 120 to 170

- `120 to 140`
  Usually unlocked by reducing silly mistakes, revising core chapters, and improving attempt quality.
- `140 to 155`
  Usually unlocked by better test analysis and stronger medium-difficulty solving.
- `155 to 170`
  Usually unlocked by subject balance, stronger PYQ pattern recognition, and cleaner time management.

### 1.6 90-Day Rescue Rules

- Do not restart from chapter 1 of every subject
- Do not over-invest in low-weightage ego chapters
- Do not measure productivity by hours alone
- Do not give mocks without analysis
- Do not study a chapter without solving questions from it

## Module 2: Weekly Execution System

### 2.1 What Toppers Actually Do Weekly

Toppers do not merely study more.

They usually do these four things better:

- Revise sooner
- Solve more targeted questions
- Analyze mistakes honestly
- Repeat a stable weekly system

### 2.2 Weekly Study Architecture

Every week should include:

- `3 days` strong focus on highest-ROI weak chapters
- `2 days` revision plus timed practice
- `1 day` mixed subject drill
- `1 day` test plus analysis

### 2.3 Weekly Planning Questions

Ask before every week:

- Which chapters can increase marks fastest this week?
- Which mistakes repeated last week?
- Which formulas, reactions, or methods are slipping?
- Where am I losing marks: knowledge, speed, or panic?

### 2.4 Daily 6-Hour Template

#### Session split

- 2 hours: Concept building on one priority chapter
- 2 hours: Question practice on the same chapter
- 1 hour: Revision of old material
- 1 hour: Mistake review and weak-area cleanup

#### Ideal use case

- School-going students
- Dropper with lower current stamina
- Students rebuilding discipline

### 2.5 Daily 10-Hour Template

#### Session split

- 2 hours: Physics concept plus problems
- 2 hours: Chemistry concept plus retention
- 2 hours: Maths problems
- 1.5 hours: PYQ block
- 1 hour: Revision block
- 1 hour: Test analysis or mistake book
- 0.5 hour: Planning next day

#### Ideal use case

- Full-time droppers
- Students already used to long study blocks

### 2.6 Weekly Review Ritual

At the end of each week, record:

- Hours studied
- Chapters advanced
- Chapters revised
- Tests given
- Accuracy percentage
- Top 5 mistakes
- Next week focus

## Module 3: PYQ Attack System

### 3.1 Why PYQs Matter

Past year questions teach the exam's real language.

They show:

- Repeated concepts
- Question framing style
- Difficulty pattern
- Traps that keep recurring

### 3.2 How To Mine PYQs Properly

Do not solve PYQs casually.

Use this sequence:

1. Pick one chapter
2. Solve PYQs chapter-wise
3. Tag each question by concept
4. Mark repeated patterns
5. Note where your method breaks
6. Re-solve missed questions after 48 hours

### 3.3 PYQ Pattern Recognition System

For every chapter, ask:

- Which 3 to 5 concepts appear most?
- What kind of shortcuts work repeatedly?
- Which forms of the question confuse me?
- What is the easiest mark in this chapter?

### 3.4 Error Tagging Method

Tag each wrong question with one of these labels:

- `C1`: Concept not understood
- `C2`: Concept known but forgotten
- `A1`: Calculation mistake
- `A2`: Misread question
- `T1`: Time pressure
- `S1`: Wrong strategy or question selection

### 3.5 PYQ Review Loop

- Day 1: Solve
- Day 2: Analyze and tag
- Day 3: Re-solve without answer support
- Day 7: Review only tagged mistakes
- Day 14: Mixed recall test from the same chapter

## Module 4: Test Analysis Framework

### 4.1 Why Students Stay Stuck

Most students give tests.
Very few extract intelligence from tests.

Marks improve when analysis becomes systematic.

### 4.2 The Test Analysis Questions

For every wrong or skipped question, answer:

- Why did I get this wrong?
- Was it a concept error?
- Was it a silly mistake?
- Was it a time-management issue?
- Did I panic or guess?
- Did I actually know this but fail to retrieve it?

### 4.3 Four-Error Diagnosis Model

- `Concept Error`
  You did not know the idea, formula, or approach.
- `Silly Mistake`
  You knew it but lost marks through avoidable carelessness.
- `Time Management Error`
  You spent too long, started too late, or attempted in the wrong order.
- `Selection Error`
  You chose the wrong question set for your current strength.

### 4.4 The Post-Test Recovery Template

Immediately after every test:

1. Record score, attempt count, accuracy
2. List all wrong questions by chapter
3. Tag the reason for each error
4. Mark which errors are repeat errors
5. Decide one correction rule for the next test

### 4.5 Gold Rule

Do not just ask, "How many marks did I get?"

Ask, "What exact mechanism made me lose the next 20 marks?"

## Module 5: What I'd Do If I Restarted JEE Today

### 5.1 The Restart Rules

If I restarted JEE prep today, I would:

- Stop collecting resources
- Pick one source per subject and stay with it
- Start PYQs much earlier
- Build a mistake book from week one
- Track revision with a visible dashboard
- Use tests as feedback, not as emotional drama

### 5.2 The Resource Rule

Too many resources create fake productivity.

Better system:

- One main concept source
- One question bank
- One PYQ source
- One revision sheet system

### 5.3 The Energy Rule

Not every day will be elite.

The student needs two modes:

- `Full-power day`
  Long, focused, problem-heavy
- `Recovery day`
  Shorter, revision-heavy, no-zero day

### 5.4 The Final Advice

The exam is not won by motivation.

It is won by:

- Repetition
- Diagnosis
- Smart chapter selection
- Timed practice
- Relentless correction
